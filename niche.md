# Niche

**Original niche: Pickleball gear & accessories (buyer-intent reviews, comparisons, and buying guides)**

**Update, 2026-07-12: the site has expanded beyond this single niche into a multi-category buying-guide site.** This file is kept as-is below for historical record of the reasoning that launched the site. For the current active categories, the taxonomy used in post front matter, and how future subtopics/categories get chosen, see `categories.md`.

## Why this niche

1. **Explosive, still-growing demand.** Pickleball is consistently ranked as one of the fastest-growing sports in the US, and the pickleball equipment market is projected to keep growing sharply through the early 2030s (multiple industry forecasts put it in the multi-billion-dollar range by 2032). Search demand for gear ("best pickleball paddle," "pickleball shoes," etc.) is durable and evergreen, not a fad that dies in a year.
2. **High buyer intent, high conversion.** Pickleball players are technical, gear-obsessed buyers who research before purchasing. Industry sources cite conversion rates of 3-8% for pickleball content, well above general sports-retail average, because people search with clear purchase intent ("best paddle for beginners under $100," "X vs Y paddle").
3. **Lower competition than mainstream sports/tech niches.** Unlike categories such as home office gear, general fitness, or consumer electronics (dominated by Wirecutter, RTINGS, CNET, etc.), pickleball is newer and less saturated by big-budget publishers, while still having dozens of legitimate gear brands (JOOLA, Selkirk, CRBN, Six Zero, Vessel, etc.) producing genuinely differentiated products worth comparing.
4. **Clean fit for Amazon Associates.** Paddles, balls, shoes, bags, nets, and apparel are standard sporting-goods products — no restricted/prohibited categories (no supplements, no health claims, no adult content, no items requiring special ungating approval).
5. **Deep, evergreen content runway.** The niche naturally supports hundreds of long-tail buyer-intent posts: skill-level guides (beginner/intermediate/advanced paddles), budget tiers, use-case guides (indoor vs outdoor, power vs control paddles), comparisons between specific models/brands, apparel and footwear, and accessories (bags, overgrips, balls, nets, ball machines).

## Rejected alternatives

- **Pet tech gadgets (GPS trackers, smart feeders, cameras).** Also a strong evergreen niche with a large and fast-growing market. Rejected because (a) it's increasingly crowded with well-funded review sites and (b) health/behavior-monitoring claims for these devices ("detects illness early," "tracks health") sit close to the kind of health-claim language that invites scrutiny — safer to avoid given the constraint against health-claim content.
- **Home office / desk setup gear (monitor arms, ergonomic chairs, keyboards).** Strong buyer intent but extremely saturated — dominated by large-budget authority sites (Wirecutter, RTINGS, The Verge) that are very hard to outrank for the highest-value comparison keywords, and ergonomic/health-related claims are common in this space, adding another reason to avoid it.

## Target reader

A recreational-to-intermediate pickleball player (often a beginner within their first 1-2 years of playing, sometimes a parent buying gear for a kid, or someone upgrading from a starter paddle) who is about to spend $30-$250 on a paddle, shoes, or accessories and wants a straightforward, honest, non-hype comparison before buying — not a competitive pro shopping for tournament-grade gear.

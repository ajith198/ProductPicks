If you share a home with a shedding dog or cat, you already know the problem with most budget robot vacuums: pet hair loves to wrap around spinning brush rollers until the robot chokes, drags itself into a corner, and stops. The good news is that a wave of genuinely capable robot vacuums has landed under the $300 mark in the last year or two, and several of them are specifically engineered around the tangle problem rather than just adding more suction and hoping for the best.

This guide focuses on what actually determines whether a robot vacuum handles pet hair well at a budget price, then points to a few real models worth considering.

## What actually matters for pet hair pickup

**Suction power (Pa) is necessary but not sufficient.** Pascals (Pa) measure how hard a vacuum can pull air and debris off the floor, and higher numbers help pull hair out of carpet fibers. But a vacuum with huge suction and a traditional bristle brush roller can still end up hopelessly tangled after a single cleaning cycle — suction alone doesn't solve the wrap-around problem.

**Brush and inlet design is the real differentiator.** Look for anti-tangle or tangle-free brush designs — dual brushes with comb teeth that continuously de-tangle hair as they spin, or, on the cheapest models, a bristle-free rubber/suction-only inlet that has nothing for hair to wrap around in the first place. This one spec matters more for pet-hair performance than the Pa rating.

**Navigation affects consistency, not just speed.** LiDAR-based robots build a real map of your home, clean in efficient rows, and remember the layout between runs — worth it if you have a larger home or multiple rooms with pets. Budget bump-and-go or gyroscope-based navigation (no laser mapping) still gets floors clean, just less methodically, and is a reasonable trade-off to hit a lower price.

**Self-emptying is a genuine convenience feature for pet owners specifically**, since pet hair fills a robot's small onboard dustbin faster than ordinary dust. A self-emptying base that holds several weeks of debris in a larger bag means you're not manually clearing a hair-clogged bin every other day.

**Battery runtime and home size.** Small apartments can get by with 90-100 minutes of runtime per charge; larger multi-room homes benefit from higher-capacity batteries or robots that can resume cleaning after a recharge.

## Before you buy: a quick checklist

- **Confirm current price.** Robot vacuum pricing swings heavily with sales — several of the models above have list prices well above $300 but regularly sell below it. Check the live listing rather than assuming a fixed price.
- **Measure your doorway thresholds and furniture clearance**, especially if you're considering a slim model like the Eufy C10 specifically for under-furniture access.
- **Check replacement part costs** (brushes, filters, mop pads, dust bags) before buying — these recurring costs matter more for pet owners who replace brushes and filters more often.
- **Read a few recent reviews for your specific flooring type** (hardwood, thick carpet, mixed) since pet-hair performance varies more by floor type than headline specs suggest.
